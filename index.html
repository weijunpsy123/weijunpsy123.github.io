Profile

A holistic view of traits, brains, and behavior.

Weijun Liu is a psychology scholar whose work connects personality, gender-typed traits, aggression, health, and neural evidence. His first-author publications appear in journals including Personality and Individual Differences, Social Neuroscience, Brain Sciences, and Frontiers in Psychiatry. Research

Core questions.

01

Gender-Typed Traits

Focuses on masculinity and femininity as psychological traits, including social and developmental factors that influence their formation, such as family structure. The work also explores psychological and behavioral differences among people with distinct gender-typed profiles.

02

Human Aggression

Investigates psychological and biological factors that shape reactive and proactive aggression, with attention to neural regulation, individual differences, and potential intervention strategies.

03

Predictive Models

Develops multivariate and holistic predictive models to understand how gender-typed traits, personality, and neural indicators jointly predict health and behavior.
